❯ Should I just record myself playing notes on the guitar on my phone?

● That's a great idea! Your own guitar would sound authentic and there's no licensing issues.

  Here's a simple approach:

  Minimal Recording (6 samples)
  Just record the 6 open strings:
  - Low E (6th string) → save as 40.wav
  - A (5th string) → save as 45.wav
  - D (4th string) → save as 50.wav
  - G (3rd string) → save as 55.wav
  - B (2nd string) → save as 59.wav
  - High E (1st string) → save as 64.wav

  Recording Tips:
  - Quiet room, phone close to sound hole
  - Let each note ring for 2-3 seconds
  - Pick/pluck at medium strength
  - Trim silence from the start of each file

  Tools to trim/convert:
  - iOS: GarageBand can export WAV
  - Or use free online tool like https://audiotrimmer.com

  Once you have the WAV files, drop them in:
  riff-engine-mobile/assets/samples/guitar/
